## Canon

Your platform's newest user is an agent acting for a stranger. Its architecture, its processes, and its support model should be discoverable and machine-readable — context a session can load, not lore a teammate transfers.

## Editor's note

Platforms were already products. Agents make them products with impatient users.

A human integrator absorbs a platform through onboarding decks, hallway answers, and a support channel's collective memory. An agent gets none of that — it gets whatever is at a fetchable address when the session starts. Teams that publish a real context pack watch consumer agents stop asking the same twenty questions; teams that don't become the support channel for everyone else's automation.

- **One pack, one address.** Architecture sketch, golden path, access process, support model — current, parseable, and versioned with the platform itself.
- **Document the process, not just the API.** Agents fail on "how do I get access" and "what happens after I file the ticket" far more than on schemas.
- **Spell out the support model.** The agent that knows when to stop and page a human is the one you want loose on your platform.
