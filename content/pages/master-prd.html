01The concept · 60,000 ft

Where in the work does AI actually help?

Teams everywhere are reinventing the same AI-augmented patterns in isolation, and good patterns die because nobody writes them down. The Playbook is the durable place those patterns live — organized so you can find the one that fits where you actually are in the work.

It answers one question, repeatedly, at every point of the product lifecycle: given what I'm doing right now, what has worked, how did they do it, and can I trust it? The lifecycle is the spine; everything hangs off it. The protocol is deliberately thin and official; the practice is rich and growing; an editor sits between the two and says what's worth your trust.

PRINCIPLE 01

Structured, not emergent

A defined 3-phase lifecycle is the backbone — not a taxonomy that sprawls from whatever gets submitted.
PRINCIPLE 02

Curated, not democratic

No reactions, votes, or leaderboards. An editor places, annotates, and endorses. Quality over volume.
PRINCIPLE 03

Three voices, never blurred

What the protocol says, what the editor thinks, and what teams actually do are always visually distinct.
02Why it exists · the stance

A backbone, on purpose

A predecessor effort was built as a neutral platform: an emergent taxonomy, no opinion about which contributions were best, a venue for others' ideas. It worked — but everything was equally weighted and the structure sprawled. This project takes the opposite stance on structure.

THE OLD WAY · NEUTRAL PLATFORM
  • Taxonomy grows organically from submissions
  • Neutral on which ideas are best
  • Community signals drive prominence
  • A venue, with little point of view
THIS PROJECT · OPINIONATED SPINE
  • A fixed lifecycle is the spine
  • An editor curates, places, and endorses
  • Community signals stripped; trust is editorial
  • Thin protocol + opinionated expansion

04The central UX idea

Three voices, unmistakable at a glance

Every substantive block of content is one of three voices, each with its own treatment. A reader must never confuse the protocol's word with the editor's opinion or with a field report. There is no literal "legend" — the distinction is carried by consistent labeling and color across the whole IA.

Canon
What the PDLC says
The official protocol. Thin, authoritative, sealed in brass. One or two sentences — and sometimes deliberately silent.
brass · serif · sealed
Editor's note
My expansion on the canon
Opinionated, bylined, and clearly the editor's — not the protocol's. On a phase page it's the section's core intro: a lead, a paragraph, and bold-led takeaways.
ink · italic serif · bylined
Practice
What teams actually do
The bulk of the library — use-case cards and the technique tags they carry. The field, in the field's words.
accent · cards · sourced

08How the thin protocol grows

Endorsement

The canon stays thin. The only way it grows is endorsement: when a practice proves itself across enough teams, the editor promotes it into the protocol's recommendations. Endorsed techniques (and exemplar use cases) carry a brass PDLC-endorsed seal. It is deliberately slow and rare.

STAGE 01
Submitted
A use case arrives via GitHub issue — a goal and an approach.
STAGE 02
Placed
The editor files it on the spine and tags its techniques.
STAGE 03
Proven
A technique recurs across teams with consistent results.
STAGE 04
Endorsed
The editor seals it; it joins the protocol's recommendations.
09Growing from the field

Contribution is GitHub issues

There is no in-app write path. A global "Submit a use case" button in the header and contextual "+ Add" links throughout (scoped to a phase, activity, or technique) all open a modal that previews the relevant issue template and deep-links to the repo. Low-ceremony, public, discussable before merge.

  • Header → Submit a use case. The front-door contribution.
  • Activity → "+ Add a use case here." Pre-scoped to that point on the spine.
  • Technique → "+ I used this — add your use case." Attach a goal/approach to an existing technique.
  • Empty states carry the same call to action — never a dead end.
Build task
Wire each modal's primary action to the repo's issue-template deep link (template + prefilled title/labels for the scope). The prototype stubs this.

13Operating model

Editorial workflow

The app reads from a curated content store. v1 ships content as a static data module (as in the prototype) and can evolve toward repo-backed Markdown/JSON.

01
Issue opened
Contributor fills a template — goal + approach.
02
Editor triages
Places on the spine, tags techniques, links resources.
03
Note written
The editor's expansion is added.
04
Merged
Appears in the library; may later be endorsed.
14Phasing

Roadmap

V1 · THIS PROTOTYPE'S SCOPE
  • The full read experience: spine, phase/activity/use-case, techniques index + pages, About
  • Three-voice system + endorsement seals
  • GitHub-issue submit affordances (modal + deep links)
  • Content as a curated static module
LATER
  • Search (client-side, then backend)
  • Repo-backed content pipeline (Markdown/JSON)
  • Technique clustering refactor — tags → families
  • Editor tooling; optional lightweight signals if ever wanted
